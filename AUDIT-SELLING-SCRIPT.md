# RankReady — Audit Selling Script
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** April 15, 2026  
**Format:** 15–20 minute discovery/close call

**Assumption:** You have run the prospect's GBP through your audit tool before this call. You have the results open and ready to screen-share. If you don't have this ready, reschedule the call — showing live data is the entire mechanism of this call.

---

## Before the Call — Prep Checklist

- [ ] Audit results pulled up and ready to screen-share
- [ ] Screenshot of their GBP saved (the current state, before any fixes)
- [ ] 3–4 specific findings identified and ranked by impact
- [ ] Their competitor's GBP checked — you know who is outranking them and why
- [ ] Stripe payment link ready to paste in chat if needed
- [ ] Calendar link ready for follow-up booking if needed
- [ ] Know which offer fits them: $199 audit, $299/mo standard, $399/mo full

---

## Call Flow

### Opening (0:00–2:00)

**Goal:** Get them talking. Do not open with a pitch. Open with a question about them.

> "Thanks for making time — really appreciate it. Before I pull up the audit, tell me a quick bit about [Business Name]: how long have you been open, and are most of your clients coming from online, word of mouth, or something else?"

**Listen for:**
- "Mostly word of mouth" → They haven't solved the online gap yet. Good sign.
- "We used to get calls from Google but it's been slow lately" → High intent. They feel the pain.
- "We have an agency handling our marketing" → Ask: "Are they working on your Google Business Profile specifically, or more the website/ads side?" (Agencies often ignore GBP.)

**Do not:** pitch, talk about yourself, or mention pricing in this phase.

---

### Transition to Audit (2:00–3:00)

> "Got it — that's helpful context. Let me pull up what I found when I looked at [Business Name]'s Google presence. Is it okay if I share my screen?"

Screen-share the audit results. Take 10 seconds of silence while it loads. Let them see it before you say anything.

---

### Walking Through the Audit (3:00–10:00)

**Rule:** Show the data, narrate the impact, ask a confirmatory question. Do this for 3–4 findings.

**Structure per finding:**
1. Point to the specific issue on screen
2. Explain what it means in plain language (not SEO jargon)
3. Explain the real-world impact (lost calls, not "low ranking score")
4. Ask a short question to confirm they understand

---

**Example walkthroughs by issue type:**

**No recent posts:**
> "So the first thing I want to show you — when you scroll down to the 'Updates' section on your profile, it's completely empty. Google uses post activity as a signal that a business is open and active. When there's nothing here for several months, Google's algorithm starts deprioritizing you in Map results. The businesses you see ranking above you on '[target keyword]'? Most of them are posting 2–4 times a month. Does that make sense?"

**Low review count:**
> "Your profile shows [X] reviews. For a business that's been open [Y] years, that's significantly below what Google expects relative to your competitors. The practice at [position #1] has 87 reviews — that review volume gap is one of the main reasons they're ranking above you even if their actual care quality isn't better than yours. Have you ever had a system for asking patients to leave a review after a visit?"

**No review responses:**
> "Here's one that almost nobody thinks about: you've never responded to a review. Google treats owner response rate as a ranking signal — it's a proxy for 'is this business engaged and trustworthy?' Your competitors who respond to every review are getting a small but consistent ranking boost you're not getting. It also just looks better to a new patient reading through reviews."

**Category mismatch:**
> "This is a technical one but it matters — your primary category is listed as '[wrong category].' Google tries to match search queries to the most specific category. Competitors using '[correct category]' as their primary are getting a relevance edge on the exact searches you want to show up for."

---

### The Gap Summary (10:00–11:00)

After walking through 3–4 findings, pause and summarize:

> "So to put this all together — you're not invisible on Google, but you're probably in position [X] for '[main keyword]', and there are [N] specific things on your profile that are holding you there. None of these are hard to fix. They just require knowing what to fix and doing it consistently."

Then ask the pivot question:

> "Is this the kind of thing you'd want to tackle, or does it feel too in the weeds for where you are right now?"

**Wait for them to answer. Do not fill the silence.**

---

### Gauging Fit and Offering the Right Package (11:00–14:00)

Listen to their response and route accordingly:

**If they say "I'd love to fix this but I don't have time / don't know how:"**
> "That's exactly what the full audit service is built for. What I do is go through every one of these findings, write you a prioritized fix plan, and either walk you through how to implement it or do it for you depending on what level you want. Want me to walk you through the options real quick?"

**If they say "I'd want to do some of this myself, but could use a clear roadmap:"**
> "Perfect — that's actually the entry point most people start with. It's a $199 one-time audit. You get a detailed report with every issue ranked by impact, exact instructions on how to fix each one, and a 30-minute call to walk through it. You take it from there. Most people find it clears up the confusion and they're able to implement in a weekend or two."

**If they say "I'm not sure, I'd need to think about it:"**
> "That's fair. What would make you feel confident it's worth doing?" (Let them tell you what they need. Don't assume.)

## The Three Offers — When to Present Each

| Offer | Price | When to present | What it includes |
|-------|-------|----------------|-----------------|
| One-time audit | $199 | Low urgency, wants DIY, tighter budget | Full audit report + priority fix plan + 30-min walkthrough call |
| Standard monthly | $299/mo | Wants done-for-you, moderate urgency | GBP management, posting, review responses, monthly report |
| Full monthly | $399/mo | High urgency, competitive market, wants everything | Everything in standard + citation building, competitor monitoring, strategy calls |

**Default to the $199 audit as entry point.** It's lower friction, gets them in the door, and upsells naturally if you deliver well. Do not push $299/mo on someone who isn't ready.

## Objection Handling

### "I already have someone handling my SEO."

> "That's totally fair — do you know if they're specifically working on your Google Business Profile, or is it more website and ads? The reason I ask is that GBP and on-site SEO are really different things. A lot of agencies focus on the website and don't touch the Profile at all — which is why I often find gaps even for businesses that are paying for SEO."

*If they confirm GBP is covered:* "Got it — sounds like you're in good hands. If you ever want a second set of eyes, I'm here." (Let it go. Do not badmouth the agency.)

---

### "What results can you guarantee?"

**Do not make a guarantee.** Be honest and direct:

> "I can't guarantee a specific ranking — anyone who does is either lying or doesn't understand how local search works. What I can tell you is: the issues we looked at today are real, they are fixable, and fixing them removes obstacles that are currently holding your profile back. The audit shows you exactly where those obstacles are. Whether you act on it determines what happens next."

---

### "I don't have the budget right now."

> "Understood — what would change that? Is it timing, or is it more that you're not sure the return would be there?"

*If timing:* "Makes sense. Would it help to do the audit now so you have the roadmap ready when you're ready to act? The $199 is one-time — there's no ongoing commitment."

*If ROI uncertainty:* "Fair. Let me ask — if fixing these issues got you one extra patient / customer / client per month, what's that worth to your business at the low end?" (Let them do the math. You're not inventing ROI, you're asking them to quantify their own.)

---

### "I need to talk to my partner / spouse / team first."

> "Of course — what's your timeline for that conversation? I ask because the audit report is something you could share with them to make the case — it's easier to explain in data than in conversation. If that would help, I can get you the free audit results to review together before you decide."

---

### "Why should I trust RankReady? You're new."

Be honest:

> "Completely fair question. RankReady is a solo operation — I built the audit tool and I do the work myself, so you're not dealing with an account manager who just relays messages to someone offshore. I don't have 10 years of client testimonials to show you. What I have is: the audit data we just looked at, which is real. And a very clear, low-risk entry point at $199. If the report isn't worth what you paid, I'll refund you."

*(Only make the refund offer if you mean it and can stand behind your work.)*

---

### "I'll think about it and get back to you."

> "Absolutely. One quick question before we hang up — is there anything specific you'd need to see or know to feel confident moving forward, or is it more just needing a day or two to decide?"

*If there's a specific concern:* address it before the call ends.  
*If it's just time:* "Totally fine. I'll send you a quick summary of what we covered today and the link if you want to grab it. What's the best email?"

---

## Close-to-Stripe Language

When they're ready to move forward, do not say "I'll send you an invoice." Close on the call.

> "Great — the easiest way to get started is a direct link. It takes about 2 minutes and then I'll get the full audit started. Want me to paste it in the chat right now?"

Paste the Stripe link directly in the video call chat or send via text/email while they're still on the phone.

**Wait in silence while they complete it.** Do not fill this moment with more talking.

After they pay:
> "Got it — you'll get a confirmation email in the next few minutes. I'll have the full report and priority fix plan to you within 48 hours. I'll also send a calendar link for the walkthrough call so we can go through it together. Any questions before we hang up?"

*This script is built for honesty-first selling. You're not convincing anyone of something false — you're showing them real data about their own business and giving them a clear, low-risk way to fix it. That's the whole pitch.*
