# RankReady Growth Plan V1
**Goal:** First 5 paying clients within 30 days. Zero to $1,000–$2,000 MRR.  
**Budget ceiling:** $200–$500 total for month 1.  
**Date written:** April 15, 2026  

---

## Brutal Reality Check First

Most growth advice is written for funded startups. RankReady is not that. Here is what is actually true:

**What will NOT work in month 1 (stop wasting time on these):**
- Organic SEO traffic. A new domain with no backlinks will not rank for "local SEO" anything meaningful for 6–12 months. Write content for conversion, not traffic.
- LinkedIn posts. You have no audience. Zero reach. Zero.
- Twitter/X "building in public." Same problem.
- Waiting for word of mouth. Word of mouth does not happen before you have clients.
- Google Ads without proof of conversion. You do not know your CVR yet. Spending $500 on ads before you know if the funnel converts is burning money.
- Cold email blasts with no personalization. 0.1% response rate. Not worth the time at this stage.

**What WILL work in month 1:**
1. Personalized outreach to businesses you can visibly see are bleeding money (their GBP is broken and you can prove it with a screenshot)
2. Referral partnerships with people who already sell to your target customer
3. Community presence in spaces where local business owners ask for help right now
4. Converting your free audit into a phone call (one-to-one, not automated)

## 1. Organic Traffic Strategy (Month 2+ Primary, Month 1 Foundation)

### What to build now even though it will not pay off until later

**Target keyword clusters (long-tail, low competition):**
- "how to improve Google Business Profile ranking" (1,200 searches/mo, low difficulty)
- "why is my business not showing on Google Maps" (800 searches/mo, high intent)
- "local SEO for [niche]: plumbers, dentists, salons" (200–400/mo each, very low difficulty)
- "Google Business Profile not getting calls" (450 searches/mo, very high intent)
- "how to respond to Google reviews to get more customers" (600 searches/mo)

**Content to write NOW (1 article per week, prioritized):**
1. "Why Your Google Business Profile Isn't Showing Up (And Exactly How to Fix It)" — target: business owners who just noticed they disappeared from Maps
2. "The 7 GBP Mistakes Costing Local Businesses 50% of Their Leads" — target: mid-funnel awareness, bridges to free audit CTA
3. "Local SEO for Plumbers: The 2026 Checklist That Actually Works" — one niche, replicate for dentists, salons, auto repair
4. "How to Get 5-Star Google Reviews Without Begging (And Without Breaking Rules)" — high search volume, very shareable among business owners
5. "What Is a Local SEO Audit and Do You Actually Need One?" — mid-funnel, pre-sells the $199 product

**Each article must:**
- Have a free audit CTA embedded at position 2 paragraphs in and at the end
- Include a screenshot of a real (anonymized) audit result as social proof
- Target one specific business type, not "all local businesses"
- Be 1,200–1,800 words. Not longer. Local business owners do not read 4,000-word posts.

**Do NOT:**
- Write about SEO theory. Write about their specific problem.
- Post to Medium, Substack, or LinkedIn articles. Own your content.
- Pay for a content writer yet. You need to understand what converts first.

## 2. Google Business Profile + Local Presence Strategy

This is critical and almost no SaaS founder does it: **RankReady itself needs an excellent GBP**.

### Set up RankReady GBP immediately if not done:
- Create a GBP under category: "Marketing Consultant" or "SEO Agency"
- Service area: your city + adjacent cities (do not go national yet)
- Description: lead with the customer's problem, not your product name
- Upload 10+ photos: screenshots of audits, before/after mock results, your workspace
- Add services: Local SEO Audit ($199), Monthly SEO Management ($299), Full Launch Package ($399)
- Post 3x per week minimum: tips, client wins (even anonymized), GBP myths debunked

### GBP posting schedule (ongoing, takes 15 min/day):
- Monday: educational tip ("Did you know responding to all reviews improves ranking?")
- Wednesday: client result or case study (even anonymized/generic is fine early)
- Friday: direct offer post with CTA to free audit

**Why this matters for acquisition, not just SEO:** When you do cold outreach, every prospect will Google you. A blank or weak GBP kills credibility instantly. A strong GBP with posts, photos, and even 3–5 reviews closes the trust gap.

**Get your first 5 GBP reviews from:** former colleagues, friends who run businesses, anyone who has seen your work. This is not gaming the system. This is social proof for real work.

## 3. Direct Outreach (Month 1 Primary Channel — This Is What Gets You Clients)

This is the only channel that will produce paying clients in the next 30 days. Everything else is infrastructure. Do this every day.

### Finding targets (30 minutes/day):

Search Google Maps for your target niches in your city and nearby cities. Filter for:
- Businesses with fewer than 10 reviews (easy win — you can visibly help them)
- Businesses with no GBP posts (check "Updates" tab — most have zero)
- Businesses with incomplete profiles (no hours, no photos, no description)
- Businesses ranking below position 3 for their obvious keyword ("plumber [city]")

Run their business name through your own free audit tool. Screenshot the results.

**Target niches by priority (ranked by willingness to pay + density of broken GBPs):**
1. Dental/orthodontic practices — high revenue per patient, understand marketing ROI
2. HVAC, plumbing, electrical contractors — rely 100% on local search for leads
3. Auto repair shops — high volume, very GBP-dependent
4. Personal injury / family law attorneys — high CLV, cost-per-lead aware
5. Med spas, salons, and aesthetic clinics — trend-sensitive, want visibility
6. Restaurants (bottom of list) — tight margins, owner is always the bottleneck, hard to convert

### The outreach message (email or contact form):

> Subject: Found something on your Google listing worth fixing — [Business Name]
>
> Hi [First Name],
>
> I ran a quick audit on [Business Name]'s Google presence and found a few things that are likely costing you calls.
>
> The biggest issue: [specific finding — e.g., "Your GBP has no posts in the last 90 days, which drops your ranking in Google Maps significantly."]
>
> I also noticed [second finding — e.g., "You have 8 reviews but no responses, which Google treats as a negative signal."]
>
> I build local SEO audits and fix plans for businesses like yours. I put together a full report on [Business Name] — happy to walk you through it on a 15-minute call if you want.
>
> No pitch, just the data. Reply and I'll send it over.
>
> — [Your Name], RankReady

**Rules:**
- Never use a template that feels like a template. Mention their business name and a specific finding every time.
- Send maximum 20 per day. Quality over volume.
- Follow up once, 3 days later, with one additional finding you "noticed." Then stop.
- Track every contact in a simple spreadsheet: Name, Business, Niche, Date Sent, Response, Status.

**What to do when they respond:**
Do not send a proposal by email. Book a call. The only goal of the email is a call.

On the call: walk them through their audit results screen-share. Show them the gap. Show them what fixing it looks like. Then offer the right-fit package. Most small business owners who see real data about their own business will convert if you are direct about the fix.

### Direct outreach targets: 20/day × 22 business days = 440 touches in month 1.
Expected response rate: 5–10% = 22–44 responses.  
Expected call conversion: 30–50% of responses = 7–22 calls.  
Expected close rate from calls: 30–40% = 2–9 paying clients.

**Conservative case = 2 clients. Realistic case = 5–7 clients.**

## 4. Partnerships (Highest Long-Term ROI, Needs 2–4 Weeks to Activate)

The fastest path to scale without ad spend is other people's trust. Find people who already sell to your exact customer and do not offer local SEO.

### Tier 1 Partners (highest priority):

**Web designers and developers serving SMBs**
- They build the website, deliver it, and move on. The business owner immediately asks "how do I get found on Google?" They have no answer.
- Find them: Clutch, local freelancer Facebook groups, Upwork profiles, Wix/Squarespace community forums
- Offer: 20% referral commission on first month of any subscription, or $40 flat per $199 audit sold
- Pitch: "You already have the relationship. I handle the SEO so you don't have to. You get a cut for the intro."

**Business coaches and consultants**
- They work with 10–30 small business owners at a time. One partner = multiple clients.
- Find them: Search "small business coach [city]" on LinkedIn and Google
- Offer: Revenue share + you'll audit all their clients free as a value-add for their program

**Bookkeepers and CPAs serving small businesses**
- They talk to every business owner during tax season about revenue and expenses. They are trusted advisors.
- Offer: You refer clients to them, they refer clients to you. No money changes hands. Pure warm intro exchange.

**Chambers of Commerce**
- Almost every small city has one. Monthly events. Business owners who are actively trying to grow.
- Tactic: Attend one event. Offer to do free 10-minute GBP audits at a table. Collect emails. Follow up the next day with the full report.
- Cost: $0–$50 for the event ticket.

### Partner outreach message:

> Hi [Name],
>
> I noticed you do web design for local businesses — I do local SEO audits and ongoing optimization for the same clients.
>
> I've been thinking about setting up a referral arrangement with a few web designers. Basically: when your clients ask about getting found on Google (and they always do), you send them my way. I pay you [X] per referral that converts.
>
> Would a 15-minute call to see if this makes sense be worth your time?

## 5. Community Marketing (Daily Habit, Zero Cost)

Local business owners ask for help in public every day. You need to be where they are.

### Where to show up:

**Facebook Groups (highest priority for SMBs):**
- Search: "[Your city] small business owners," "[Your city] entrepreneurs," "[Niche] business owners [region]"
- Join 8–12 relevant groups
- Rule: Never post a sales pitch. Post answers to questions about local marketing/Google/reviews.
- When someone posts "why is my Google listing not showing up?" — answer the question fully, then mention you built a tool for this and offer to run their audit free.
- Spend 20 minutes/day reading and replying. Do this every day.

**Reddit:**
- r/smallbusiness (800K members) — post answers to local SEO questions, never pitch
- r/entrepreneur — same approach
- r/Entrepreneur has weekly "help me" threads — be first to respond to anyone mentioning Google/local/maps
- Occasional post: "I built a free GBP audit tool — happy to run it for anyone here this week" — do this once a month, not weekly

**Nextdoor for Business:**
- Free to join as a local business
- Post local SEO tips targeting business owners
- Offer free audits in your neighborhood/city

**Alignable:**
- LinkedIn for small business owners. Very low competition for attention.
- Post weekly. Answer questions daily. Send 5 connection requests/day with a note referencing their industry.

## 6. Paid Traffic Tests (Start Week 3, Not Week 1)

Do not run paid ads until you have:
1. At least 2 paying clients (proof the funnel converts)
2. 20+ free audit completions (proof people engage with the tool)
3. A clear offer that worked in outreach (you know which angle closes)

### Test 1: Google Search Ads ($5/day budget = $150/month)

**Target keywords:**
- "local SEO audit" — high intent, direct match to your offer
- "Google Business Profile optimization service" — buyer intent
- "fix my Google listing" — emergency-level intent
- "local SEO for [niche]" — one niche at a time to test what converts

**Ad → Landing page:** Send traffic to a stripped-down landing page (not the homepage) with one CTA: "Get Your Free Audit." No pricing, no navigation, no distractions.

**Success threshold:** If you spend $150 and get 0 audit completions, the landing page is broken, not the ads. If you get audits but no paid conversions, the follow-up is broken.

### Test 2: Facebook/Instagram Retargeting ($3/day = $90/month)

- Retarget everyone who visited your site but did not start the audit
- Ad: "You started looking — here is what your Google listing is actually missing"
- Do not run cold Facebook traffic. Too expensive for this budget.

### What to track:
- Cost per free audit completion
- Audit completion → call booked rate
- Call → close rate
- Cost per paying client acquired

**Kill any campaign that spends $50 without a conversion event (audit completion or call booked). Do not wait.**

## 7. Lead-Gen Workflow (The Whole System)

Every piece of this plan feeds into one workflow. Build this before you do any outreach.

```
PROSPECT FOUND (GBP audit, outreach, community, ads)
         |
         v
FREE AUDIT COMPLETED or OUTREACH RESPONSE
         |
         v
PERSONALIZED FOLLOW-UP within 24 hours
(email or DM: specific findings, specific fix, CTA = 15-min call)
         |
         v
15-MINUTE DISCOVERY CALL
(screen-share their audit, show them the gap, ask: "is this a problem you want to solve?")
         |
         v
RIGHT-FIT OFFER
$199 one-time if: they want to DIY, low urgency
$299/mo if: they want it done, moderate urgency
$399/mo if: they need everything, high urgency / competitive market
         |
         v
CLOSE via Stripe link on call (not after, not by email — on the call)
         |
         v
DELIVER WITHIN 48 HOURS
(audit report + fix plan for $199; first GBP posts + report for $299/$399)
         |
         v
ASK FOR REFERRAL AT DAY 14 and DAY 30
("Is there anyone else you know who has the same problem?")
```

### Non-negotiable workflow rules:
- Always try to get a phone number, not just an email. Calls close 5x better.
- Send the Stripe link during the call. "Want to get started right now?" works more often than you think.
- Do not offer a discount. Offer more value instead (add a competitor analysis, add one extra deliverable).
- Set delivery expectations before they pay. Surprises kill referrals.

|--------|--------------|
| Paying clients | 5 |
| MRR added | $1,000+ |
| One-time revenue | $400+ |

**Funnel health ratios to watch:**
- Outreach → Response: target 5–10%

If any ratio drops below the floor, that step is broken. Fix it before scaling volume.

## 10. Honest Assessment: What Will Probably Happen

**Likely scenario (based on direct outreach math and typical SMB sales cycles):**

- Days 1–7: Lots of sending, zero responses. This is normal. Stay in.
- Days 8–14: 3–8 responses. 1–2 calls booked. Possibly 0 closes. Fix your call script.
- Days 15–21: 1–3 closes. First revenue. Confidence increases. Outreach quality improves because you know what works.
- Days 22–30: Momentum. Referrals from first clients. Partnership starting to produce. 2–5 total paying clients.

**The most common failure mode:** Spending week 1 perfecting the website instead of sending outreach. The website is good enough. Start talking to humans on day 1.

**Second most common failure mode:** Giving up after day 10 with no responses. The math says you need 100–200 touches before you statistically should expect 5+ closes. Be patient and be consistent.

**What beats everything in month 1:** One great client who sees real results and refers two more. Prioritize delivery quality above everything else once you have a paying client.
