## Why Outbound First

SEO takes 6–12 months. Inbound content takes an audience you don't have. Paid ads require conversion data you don't have yet. Direct, personalized outbound to businesses with visible GBP problems is the only channel that produces revenue in the next 30 days. Everything else is infrastructure or month-2 work.

## 1. Target Niche Order

Work these in sequence. Do not split your attention across all five at once. Dominate one, then move to the next.

### Tier 1: Dental / Orthodontic Practices

**Start here. This is your Week 1 focus.**

**Why first:**
- Average revenue per patient: $1,500–$8,000. They understand marketing ROI.
- Decision-maker is usually the practice owner or office manager — one call gets a yes/no.
- GBP problems are rampant and visibly diagnosable (missing services, low reviews, no posts).
- They pay for services without lengthy procurement cycles.
- "New patient acquisition" is a phrase they already think in — you can speak their language directly.

**What to look for when prospecting:**
- Fewer than 25 Google reviews for a practice open more than 2 years
- No GBP posts in last 60 days
- Missing "New Patients" in services
- No photo of the actual office interior
- "Orthodontist [city]" searches where they appear below position 3

---

### Tier 2: HVAC / Plumbing / Electrical Contractors

**Week 2 focus.**

**Why second:**
- These businesses live and die by local search. An HVAC company that doesn't show up on Google Maps for "AC repair [city]" is losing $50,000+ in summer revenue.
- High urgency = faster close. They feel the pain acutely.
- Owner usually answers the phone (single-location, owner-operated).
- Decision cycle: 1 call, 1 day. No committees.

**What to look for:**
- No GBP posts (most have zero)
- Few or no photos of work / trucks / team
- Responding to zero reviews
- Showing up position 4–10 when competitors have 3x more reviews

---

### Tier 3: Auto Repair Shops

**Week 2–3 focus (layer in alongside HVAC).**

**Why third:**
- High density of shops — lots of prospects in any market.
- Independent shops are 100% local-search dependent.
- Owner is on-site, approachable, and makes decisions fast.
- Low sophistication = easy wins. Most auto shops have never thought about GBP optimization.

**What to look for:**
- Shops that haven't responded to a single review
- Missing business hours or holiday hours that cause "temporarily closed" signals
- Generic business name photos only, no photos of actual work or staff
- No mention of specific services (brakes, tires, oil change) in GBP attributes

---

### Tier 4: Personal Injury / Family Law Attorneys

**Week 3 focus.**

**Why fourth:**
- Very high CLV — one signed client = $5,000–$50,000+ in revenue for them.
- They are cost-per-lead aware and will pay for quality optimization.
- Slightly longer decision cycle (may involve a partner), but close rates are high once you get the right person on the phone.
- Warning: solo practitioners and small 2–5 attorney firms only. Mid-size and large firms have marketing departments — you'll get stuck in procurement.

**What to look for:**
- "Personal injury attorney [city]" or "divorce lawyer [city]" — find positions 4–10
- Low review count relative to years in practice
- GBP description written in legalese, not for searchers
- No Q&A section populated

---

### Tier 5: Med Spas / Salons / Aesthetic Clinics

**Week 3–4 as fill-in or pivot if other niches underperform.**

**Why fifth:**
- Trend-sensitive owners who understand the value of visibility.
- Services are high-margin, repeat-purchase — they will pay for ongoing plans.
- Often Instagram-focused and have neglected Google entirely. Easy gap to show.
- Decision-maker is usually the owner. Fast cycles.

**Note:** Avoid franchise locations (Great Clips, European Wax Center, etc.) — they don't control their GBP and can't buy.

## 2. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Do not pitch everyone. Pitch this person.**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Business type | Single-location or 2–3 location independent business in a high-CLV service niche |
| Geography | Operates in a mid-size or large metro where local search competition exists |
| Revenue | $300K–$5M/year. Small enough that the owner is reachable, large enough to pay. |
| GBP status | Has a GBP, but it's incomplete, low on reviews, or has visible gaps your audit catches |
| Owner posture | Owner answers email or the phone, or there is a named manager who does |
| Pain | Getting outranked by competitors with similar or worse actual quality |
| Sophistication | Has heard of SEO, maybe paid for it before, is skeptical but open if you show data |
| NOT a fit | Franchise/corporate locations, businesses with no GBP, businesses ranking #1 already, restaurants (tight margins, wrong CLV) |

**Green flags in your research:**
- You can find the owner's name or direct email easily
- They have responded to some reviews (shows they care about reputation)
- They have at least 1 Google photo (shows some GBP awareness)
- They are in a competitive market (search their main keyword, see 3+ active competitors)

**Red flags — skip these:**
- No GBP at all (too much education required)
- Franchise or chain with a corporate marketing team
- Negative Glassdoor reviews about ownership being unstable
- Business closed or "temporarily closed" on GBP

## 3. Lead Qualification Rubric

Run every prospect through this before spending time on outreach. Score quickly — 2 minutes max.

| Criterion | 0 points | 1 point | 2 points |
|-----------|----------|---------|---------|
| GBP is verified and active | No GBP | Unverified | Verified and active |
| Review count vs. years open | 0–5 reviews | 6–20 reviews | 20+ reviews but clearly gaps |
| Last GBP post | Never or 6+ months ago | 2–6 months ago | 1–2 months ago (still weak) |
| Photo quality/count | 0–3 generic photos | 4–10 mixed | 10+ but not optimized |
| Response to reviews | Responds to none | Responds to some | Responds to most |
| Ranking position (primary keyword) | Not ranking in top 20 | Position 7–20 | Position 4–6 |
| Can find owner contact | No | Email via contact form | Direct email or phone |

**Total score range: 0–14**

- **10–14:** Warm lead. Specific visible issues. High outreach priority.
- **6–9:** Viable. Run the audit, craft a personalized message.
- **0–5:** Skip or deprioritize. Problems may be too structural for a quick win.

**Assumption:** A business scoring 10+ has enough visible pain that your outreach email can mention 2–3 specific, real issues. That specificity is what gets responses.

## 4. Funnel Stages

Keep it simple. A prospect is in exactly one stage at any time.

| Stage | Definition | Your Job |
|-------|------------|---------|
| **PROSPECT** | Found, qualified, not yet contacted | Score them, run the audit, prep personalized message |
| **CONTACTED** | Email/DM/form sent, awaiting response | Wait 3 days, then follow up once |
| **RESPONDED** | They replied (positive or question) | Reply within 2 hours. Goal: book call. |
| **CALL BOOKED** | Discovery call scheduled | Send calendar confirmation, prep their audit summary |
| **CALL DONE** | Call happened | Same-day follow-up. If no close, next step agreed. |
| **PROPOSAL SENT** | Stripe link or offer sent post-call | Follow up in 24 hours if no action |
| **CLOSED - WON** | Paid | Deliver within 48 hours. Ask for referral at Day 14. |
| **CLOSED - LOST** | Hard no | Note objection for future. Re-contact in 90 days if appropriate. |
| **NURTURE** | Soft no ("not now") | 30-day follow-up with a new finding or relevant tip |

## 5. Daily and Weekly Activity Targets

These are the minimum inputs required to hit 5 clients in 30 days. If you hit these numbers, the math works. If you don't, the math doesn't.

### Daily targets (every business day)

| Activity | Target | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| New prospects identified and scored | 25 | Google Maps search, 30 min |
| Outreach messages sent (email/form/DM) | 15–20 | Personalized. Not blasted. |
| Follow-ups sent (Day 3 sequence) | Varies by pipeline | All Day-3 contacts get follow-up |
| Community replies (FB groups, Reddit) | 2–3 | Answer a question, no pitch |
| Inbound responses handled | All within 2 hours | This is your highest-priority task |

### Weekly targets

| Activity | Week 1 | Week 2 | Week 3 | Week 4 |
|---------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Total outreach sent | 50 | 80 | 80 | 80 |
| Follow-ups sent | 10 | 30 | 40 | 40 |
| Partner contacts | 3 | 5 | 5 | 3 |
| Discovery calls booked | 1 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
| Discovery calls completed | 0–1 | 2–3 | 3–4 | 4–5 |
| Paid clients closed | 0 | 1–2 | 2–3 | 1–2 |

### Time budget per day (2–3 hours total)

| Block | Time | Activity |
|-------|------|---------|
| Morning (30 min) | 8–8:30am | Respond to all inbound. Handle any call bookings. |
| Prospecting block (45 min) | 9–9:45am | Find and score 25 new leads in target niche |
| Outreach block (60 min) | 10–11am | Write and send 15–20 personalized messages |
| Community block (20 min) | Lunch or evening | Reply to 2–3 posts in SMB groups |
| Follow-up block (15 min) | End of day | Send Day-3 follow-ups to all contacts due |

| Outreach messages sent | 15 | 80 |
| Response rate | — | 5–10% |
| Follow-ups sent | all Day-3 due | — |

### Outcome KPIs (track weekly)

| Metric | 30-day target |
|--------|--------------|
| Total outreach touches | 350–400 |
| Responses received | 20–40 |
| Discovery calls completed | 10–15 |
| Paid clients | 5 |
| Revenue (one-time + MRR) | $1,500–$2,500 |
| Cost per client | <$20 (time only) |

### Funnel health ratios — these tell you what's broken

| Ratio | Target | If below target... |
|-------|--------|--------------------|
| Outreach → Response | 5–10% | Subject line or specificity is weak. Rewrite. |
| Response → Call booked | 30–50% | You're over-pitching in replies. Go shorter. |
| Call → Close | 30–40% | Call script needs work. Practice the close. |
| Audit completion → Call booked | 10–20% | CTA on results page or follow-up needs to be sharper. |

### Weekly review checklist (Friday, 15 minutes)

- [ ] Total outreach sent this week vs. target?
- [ ] Response rate this week?
- [ ] Calls booked this week?
- [ ] Any pattern in who responded vs. who didn't?
- [ ] Any objection heard twice? Needs a new rebuttal.
- [ ] What niche is performing? Double it next week.
- [ ] What niche is flat? Drop or reprioritize.
