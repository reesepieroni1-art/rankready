# RankReady — Outreach Scripts
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** April 15, 2026

**How to use these:** These are starting points, not copy-paste templates. Every message you send should include at least one real, specific finding about that exact business. Generic messages get 0% response rates. Personalized ones with a specific observation get 5–15%.

**The rule:** If you could send this exact message to 100 different businesses without changing a word, it is too generic. Rewrite it.

---

## Script 1 — Cold Email (Full Version)

**When to use:** When you have a verified email address and 2–3 specific GBP findings from running the audit.

**Target length:** 150–180 words. Not a word more.

---

**Subject line options (test these — pick one per niche batch):**
- `Found something on your Google listing — [Business Name]`
- `[Business Name] — one issue on your Google profile worth fixing`
- `Quick note about [Business Name]'s Google Maps ranking`
- `Why [Business Name] might be losing calls to [Competitor Name]`

---

**Body:**

> Hi [First Name],
>
> I ran a quick audit on [Business Name]'s Google Business Profile and found a few things that are likely costing you calls each month.
>
> The biggest issue: [Specific finding — e.g., "Your profile hasn't had a single post in over 90 days. Google's algorithm treats this as a signal that the business is inactive, which drops your ranking in Maps."]
>
> I also noticed [Second specific finding — e.g., "You have 14 reviews but haven't responded to any of them. Google uses response rate as a local ranking factor — your competitors who respond are outranking you on that signal alone."]
>
> I do local SEO audits and fix plans for businesses like yours — I actually put together a report on [Business Name] that I'd share on a quick 15-minute call if you want to see it.
>
> No pitch. Just the data and what I'd fix.
>
> — [Your Name]  
> RankReady | rankready.com  
> [Phone]

---

**Notes:**
- Do not attach anything. Attachments get flagged as spam.
- Do not mention pricing in the first email.
- "No pitch, just the data" is not false — the call is genuinely about showing them the audit first.
- Use "calls" not "leads" or "traffic" — business owners think in calls.

## Script 2 — Cold Email (Short Version)

**When to use:** When you only have one strong finding, or when you are reaching out through a contact form that truncates long messages.

---

**Subject:** `[Business Name] — noticed this on your Google profile`

> Hi [First Name],
>
> I ran an audit on [Business Name]'s Google Business Profile and found one issue that's likely suppressing your Maps ranking: [specific finding in one sentence].
>
> I put together a short summary. Worth a 10-minute call?
>
> — [Your Name], RankReady

---

**Notes:**
- 3 sentences. That's the whole email.
- Works surprisingly well because it looks like something a human actually wrote.
- The specific finding is the entire reason this gets opened. Do not skip it.

## Script 3 — Cold DM / Contact Form Version

**When to use:** When email isn't available and you're reaching through Facebook DMs, Instagram DMs, LinkedIn, or the business's website contact form.

**Constraint:** Keep it even shorter than email. DMs that look like form letters get ignored instantly.

---

> Hi [First Name] — quick note on [Business Name]'s Google profile.
>
> I ran an audit on it and found [specific issue in one phrase, e.g., "no posts in 6 months"]. That's one of the bigger ranking factors Google uses for Maps right now.
>
> I put together a short report on it — happy to share if useful. Is email better for you, or should I just drop it here?

---

**Notes:**
- The closing question ("email or here?") invites a low-friction reply.
- Do not lead with "I'm from RankReady" — that reads like a pitch.
- If they ask what you do: "I help local businesses fix their Google Business Profile so they rank higher in Maps and get more calls. I do a paid audit for $199 or a quick free version."

## Script 4 — Follow-Up #1 (Day 3)

**When to use:** 3 business days after the first outreach with no response.

**Do not:** Apologize for following up. Do not say "just circling back." Add something new.

---

**Subject:** `[Business Name] — one more thing I noticed`

> Hi [First Name],
>
> Wanted to follow up on my note from [day of week].
>
> One more thing I found when I dug into [Business Name]'s profile: [new specific finding — different from what you mentioned in email #1, e.g., "Your business category is listed as 'General Contractor' but competitors ranking above you are listed as 'HVAC Contractor' — Google prioritizes exact category matches for local searches."].
>
> Still happy to walk you through the full report on a quick call. Just reply and we'll find a time.
>
> — [Your Name], RankReady

---

**Notes:**
- New finding = new reason to respond. You are not repeating yourself.
- This email is often shorter than the first. That's fine.
- After this follow-up, you have sent 2 messages. One more is reasonable. More than 3 is noise.

---

## Script 5 — Follow-Up #2 (Day 7–10, Final)

**When to use:** 4–7 days after Follow-Up #1. This is your last contact unless they signal interest.

**Goal:** Give them an easy out and leave a good impression. You may want to reach back in 60–90 days.

---

**Subject:** `Last note on [Business Name]'s Google profile`

> Hi [First Name],
>
> Last message from me on this — I don't want to clog your inbox.
>
> If fixing [Business Name]'s Google presence isn't a priority right now, totally understand. I'll leave you alone.
>
> If it ever comes back up, I'm at [email] / [phone]. The free audit is still at rankready.com if you want to pull the data yourself.
>
> — [Your Name]

---

**Notes:**
- "Last message from me" reduces friction — many people respond to this one because the pressure is gone.
- Leaving the door open without begging for a response is the right tone.
- Move them to NURTURE stage. Set a 60-day reminder to send one more message with a new angle.

## Script 6 — Referral Partner Outreach

**When to use:** Reaching out to web designers, business coaches, CPAs, or bookkeepers who serve your exact customer.

**Tone:** Peer-to-peer. You are proposing a mutual arrangement, not asking for a favor.

---

**Subject:** `Referral arrangement — you do web design, I do local SEO`

> Hi [First Name],
>
> I came across your work — you do web design for local businesses, and I do Google Business Profile optimization and local SEO audits for the same type of clients.
>
> The gap I keep running into: business owners get a new site, ask "how do I show up on Google," and have no one to hand them off to. That's usually the moment you finish and move on.
>
> I'm setting up a referral arrangement with a few web designers in [city/region]. Basic structure: when a client asks you about local search visibility, you send them my way. I pay you $40 per $199 audit that converts, or 20% of the first month of any ongoing plan.
>
> Nothing to sign. Just a mutual arrangement that makes both of us more useful to our clients.
>
> Worth a 15-minute call to see if there's a fit?
>
> — [Your Name]  
> RankReady | rankready.com

---

**Notes:**
- Replace "web designer" and adjust commission accordingly for CPAs, coaches, etc.
- Do not over-engineer the referral structure upfront. Keep it simple in the first message.
- CPA/bookkeeper version: replace commission with a straight swap — "I refer clients to you for bookkeeping, you refer clients to me for local SEO. No money changes hands."

## Script 7 — Voicemail Script

**When to use:** When you have a direct number and want to leave a message. Keep it under 30 seconds.

**Practice this before you call.** Stumbling through a voicemail tanks your credibility.

---

> "Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from RankReady. I left a quick email about [Business Name]'s Google Business Profile — I found a couple of things on it that are likely suppressing your ranking in Maps. I put together a short report on it if you'd like to see it. My number is [phone]. Happy to walk you through it in 10 minutes. Again, [your name], [phone]. Thanks."

---

**Notes:**
- State your first name and company once at the start, once at the end.
- Mention "Google Business Profile" and "Maps" — not "SEO." Business owners know what Maps is.
- Do not mention pricing in the voicemail.
- If they call back: do not sell on that call. Schedule the discovery call. "Let me pull up your report and walk you through it — when are you free for 15 minutes?"

## Personalization Cheat Sheet — What to Look For in 5 Minutes

Before writing any outreach, open Google Maps and search "[business name] [city]." Spend 5 minutes looking for:

| Signal | How to spot it | What to say in outreach |
|--------|----------------|-------------------------|
| No recent posts | Click "Updates" tab — blank or 6+ months old | "Your profile hasn't had a post in [X] months — Google treats this as a low-activity signal." |
| Low reviews relative to age | Under 15 reviews for 3+ year business | "You've been open [X] years and have [Y] reviews — competitors with 40+ reviews are ranking above you on that factor alone." |
| No review responses | Click any review — "Owner" has no responses | "You haven't responded to any of your [N] reviews — Google factors response rate into local ranking." |
| Incomplete category | "Contractor" instead of "HVAC Contractor" | "Your primary category is too broad — competitors ranking above you use a more specific category." |
| Missing services list | Services tab empty | "Your services section is empty — searchers and Google both use this to confirm relevance for specific searches." |
| Few/no interior photos | Only 1–2 stock-looking images | "You have [N] photos, all exterior shots — adding interior and team photos typically increases profile engagement by 30–40%." |
