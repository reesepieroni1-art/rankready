# RankReady — Week 1 Execution Plan
**Goal for the week:** First call booked. 50 outreach messages sent. System operational.  
**Date:** Week of April 15, 2026  
**Constraint:** Solo operator. Focus beats volume.

---

## What Week 1 Is Actually For

You will not close a client this week. That is fine. Week 1 is about:
1. Getting the prospecting and outreach system running
2. Sending enough messages that you get your first 1–3 responses
3. Booking at least one discovery call

If you end Week 1 with 50+ outreach messages sent and one call booked, the week was a success. If you spend the week tweaking your website, it wasn't.

---

## Day 1 — Monday, April 15: System Setup (3 hours)

The goal today is to build the machine, not run it. You only set this up once.

### Morning block (90 minutes)

**1. Set up the lead tracker**
- Open LEAD-TRACKER.csv in Google Sheets (upload it there)
- Read the headers — understand what each column is for
- You will fill in one row per prospect before sending any outreach

**2. Set up your outreach inbox**
- If using Gmail: create a label called "RankReady Outreach" and set up a filter so all replies to your outreach emails get that label
- Pin this label so you see it immediately when you open Gmail
- Response time goal: reply to any inbound within 2 hours during business hours

**3. Set up Calendly (if not already done)**
- Create one event type: "15-Minute Google Profile Review Call"
- Set available windows: Mon–Fri, whatever 2-hour blocks work for you
- Add the meeting description: "I'll pull up your Google Business Profile audit and walk you through what I found. No pitch — just the data."
- Copy the booking link — you will paste this in responses

**4. Verify your audit tool works**
- Go to rankready.com, run the free audit on a real local business
- Make sure results load correctly and look credible
- Screenshot the results so you know what they look like from a client's perspective

### Afternoon block (90 minutes)

**5. Run audits on 10 dental practices in your target market**
- Google Maps: search "dentist [your city]" and "orthodontist [your city]"
- For each one that looks promising: run the audit, screenshot results, note the top 2 findings
- Score each one using the rubric in OUTBOUND-OPERATING-SYSTEM.md
- Pick the top 5 scorers. These are your first outreach targets.

**6. Fill out 5 rows in the lead tracker**
- Business name, contact info, GBP score, key issues, audit run = Y
- Do not send any outreach yet. You send tomorrow when you can do it properly.

**End of Day 1 checkpoint:**
- [ ] Lead tracker is live in Google Sheets
- [ ] Calendly is set up with the right event type
- [ ] Audit tool tested and working
- [ ] 5 scored, audit-run dental prospects in the tracker
- [ ] You know what your audit results look like

## Day 2 — Tuesday, April 16: First Outreach (2.5 hours)

The goal today is to send 10 personalized messages to the 5 targets from yesterday, plus 5 more you prospect this morning.

### Morning block (60 minutes)

**Prospect 5 more dental targets**
- Same process as yesterday: Google Maps search, run audit, screenshot, score
- Fill out 5 more rows in the tracker
- You now have 10 prospects ready to contact

### Outreach block (90 minutes)

**Write and send 10 emails — one at a time**

For each email:
1. Open the prospect's Google Maps listing
2. Pull up your audit results for them
3. Open OUTREACH-SCRIPTS.md — use Script 1 (full) or Script 2 (short) as your base
4. Personalize: fill in their name, business name, and 2 real findings
5. Read it out loud before sending. If it sounds like a template, rewrite it.
6. Send. Log in the tracker: Stage = CONTACTED, Outreach Date = today, channel, subject line used.

**Time budget per email: 8–9 minutes. Do not spend 20 minutes on one email.**

### End of Day 2 checkpoint:
- [ ] 10 outreach emails sent
- [ ] All 10 logged in tracker
- [ ] Inbox monitored — any responses handled within 2 hours

## What to Do If You Get a Response This Week

Drop everything else and handle it within 2 hours. A response is 10x more valuable than sending another cold message.

**If they express interest:**
> Reply immediately, propose 2 specific times for a 15-minute call, or paste your Calendly link. One sentence. No fluff.

**If they ask "what exactly do you do?":**
> "I run local SEO audits for businesses like yours — I found [specific issue] on your Google Business Profile and put together a report on it. Easiest to just show you on a quick call. Are you free Thursday or Friday for 15 minutes?"

**If they say "not right now":**
> Move them to NURTURE in the tracker. Set a 30-day reminder. Reply: "Totally understand — I'll check back in a month. In the meantime, the free version of the audit is at rankready.com if you want to pull the data yourself."

## What Not to Do This Week

- Do not rebuild the website. It is good enough.
- Do not write blog posts. That is month-2 work.
- Do not spend more than 30 minutes total on LinkedIn, Twitter, or Instagram.
- Do not send 100 generic emails. 50 personalized ones will outperform 100 generic ones by a factor of 10.
- Do not panic if Day 1–4 produce zero responses. That is normal. The response rate is a weekly metric, not a daily one.
- Do not offer discounts. If someone pushes back on price, add value (more deliverables), not a lower price.
